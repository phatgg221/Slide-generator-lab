# Style & Layout Guidance

Rules for structuring a deck and choosing slide layouts/variants. Followed by
the planning and variant-selection agents. Keep edits crisp.

## Deck structure
- **Sandwich the deck**: strong opener (title), a clear narrative middle, a
  closing summary. Start with `title`, end with `summary`/`closing` when present.
- **One idea per slide.** If a slide is trying to say two things, split it.
- **Match depth to content.** Don't pad to hit a slide count; don't cram.
- **Vary the rhythm.** Alternate text-heavy and visual/stat slides; avoid three
  identical layouts in a row.

## Choosing a layout / variant
- Pick the layout whose slots match the data: 2–3 numbers → a stat/KPI layout;
  4+ items → a list/bullets layout; a comparison → a two-column layout; a single
  striking line → a quote layout.
- Prefer a variant with the **right number of slots** over a prettier one that
  doesn't fit — empty or overflowing slots look broken.
- Use stat/chart layouts only when there are real numbers to show.

## Writing within a layout
- **Titles**: punchy, 2–6 words. **Section headers**: a short phrase.
  **Body/bullets**: tight, parallel phrasing, not full paragraphs.
- **Respect character budgets** — going slightly under is good; over breaks the
  design. Front-load the important words (they survive truncation).
- **Left-align** body text and lists; center only titles/short callouts.

## Avoid (reads as AI-generated filler)
- Generic openers ("In today's fast-paced world…").
- Walls of text; full sentences where a phrase works.
- Repeating the slide title inside its own body.
- Every slide the same shape.
